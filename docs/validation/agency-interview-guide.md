# Agency Interview Guide

Goal: validate whether NZ/AU agencies will pay for AI-native, evidence-cited reporting before investing deeply in every connector.

## Target sample

- 10 to 15 interviews.
- Mix of solo consultants, 5 to 25-client agencies, and 50+ client performance teams.
- Prioritize teams already using Meta, Google Ads, GA4, GSC, TikTok, or LinkedIn reporting.

## Core questions

1. How many hours does your team spend on recurring client reports each month?
2. Which channels are must-have for the first version?
3. What makes a report client-ready in your agency?
4. Where do existing tools fail: setup, pricing, data quality, insight quality, or design?
5. Would evidence-cited AI recommendations change the workflow, or just create review work?
6. What is the most defensible pricing metric: client count, spend under management, seats, or reports sent?
7. What would make you trust or reject an AI-generated recommendation?

## Prototype tasks

- Ask the interviewee to open `/dashboard`, find the highest-risk client issue, and explain what they would do next.
- Ask them to open `/reports`, decide if the builder is enough for a first client-ready report, and name missing blocks.
- Ask them to inspect `/connectors` and rank connector priority.

## Decision gates

- Continue if at least 8 of 15 agencies report 5+ hours/month of painful reporting work.
- Continue if at least 5 agencies say cited recommendations are a buying trigger.
- Defer a connector if fewer than 3 agencies name it as critical for launch.
